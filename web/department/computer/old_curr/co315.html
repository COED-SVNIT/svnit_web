  • Introduction

    • (12 Hours)

    Sets, Random Experiments, Events, Probability: Conditional Probability, Bayes’ rule, Random Variables, Probability Distributions and their properties; Binomial Distribution, Bernoulli Trials, Normal Distribution, Poisson Distribution, Joint Distributions, Expectation, Functions of Random Variables, Moments, Moment Generating Functions, Law of Large Numbers, Central Limit Theorem.

  • Sampling Theory

    • (06 Hours)

    Statistical Inference, Random Samples, Sampling Distribution, Sample Mean, Variance and other statistics, Estimation Theory: Unbiased Estimates, Point Estimates and Interval Estimates, Confidence Interval, Maximum Likelihood Estimates
  • Hypotheses Testing

    • (08 Hours)

    Statistical Decisions, Statistical Hypotheses and Significance, Type I and Type II Errors, Level of Significance, Test Involving Normal Distribution and Chi-square Distribution, One-Tailed and Two-Tailed Tests, Estimation Theory and Hypotheses Testing
  • Stochastic Processes and Applications

    • (16 Hours)

    Random Walk, Brownian Motion, Poisson process, Mean Square Estimation, Entropy, Queuing Theory, Discrete Time Markov Chains, Continuous Time Markov Chains, Q-matrix, Birth-death processes, Quasi birth death processes, Applications: Performance Analysis of Computer Network, Model Based Computer Vision.

    (Total Contact Time: 42 Hours)
